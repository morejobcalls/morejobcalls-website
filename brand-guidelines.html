01 — Brand Identity

Who we are & why it matters

Before colors and fonts, brand is built on a clear point of view. This is ours.

More Job Calls exists to give home service contractors a predictable, exclusive source of inbound calls — so the best tradespeople win more work, every single month.

Brand Personality

Five words that define every piece of content we produce. When something feels off, check it against these.

Authoritative
We know lead generation for contractors. We lead, we don't follow.
Direct
Contractors are busy. Say what you mean. Cut the fluff.
Results-Driven
Every claim we make is tied to a real, measurable outcome.
Trustworthy
We don't oversell. We under-promise and over-deliver.
Human
We're talking to real people who run real businesses — not corporations.

Target Audience

Our content speaks to owners and operators of home service businesses. Know them deeply.

Primary — Owner-Operators
  • HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical, Roofing, Landscaping
  • 1–10 employees, owner actively involved in the work
  • Wants consistent, high-quality job calls — not tire-kickers
  • Skeptical of marketing agencies, has been burned before
  • Values exclusivity — doesn't want to compete for the same lead
Secondary — Growing Operations
  • 10–50 employees, dedicated office staff
  • Looking to scale specific service lines or territories
  • Cares about cost-per-job and ROI tracking
  • May be comparing with in-house marketing teams

Color Usage Rules

Color Use it for Never use it for
Ivory Page backgrounds, section alternates, form backgrounds Text (too low contrast), button fills, important UI states
Charcoal All body text, headlines on light backgrounds, dark sections/footers Text on dark backgrounds (use white), decorative elements
Blueprint Blue Primary CTAs, links, nav highlights, feature icons, trust elements Body text, backgrounds covering large areas, error states
Job Gold Urgency badges, stat callouts, testimonial highlights, the one accent that demands attention Primary CTAs (use blue), backgrounds of large areas, body text
♿
Accessibility: White or Ivory text on Blueprint Blue (#4D87C8) passes WCAG AA at 16px+. Charcoal on Ivory passes AAA. Always check contrast before using new combinations — Job Gold (#F5C518) requires dark (Charcoal) text to be legible.

Logo Do's & Don'ts

✓ DO

Use the approved color versions above — light, dark, or blue background.

✓ DO

Maintain minimum clearspace equal to the cap-height of "M" on all sides.

✓ DO

Scale proportionally. Never stretch or compress the wordmark.

✓ DO

Minimum size: 120px wide on screen, 1 inch wide in print.

✕ DON'T

Don't place the logo on busy photography without an overlay.

✕ DON'T

Don't recolor the wordmark in Job Gold on a light background (low contrast).

✕ DON'T

Don't add drop shadows, outlines, gradients, or effects to the wordmark.

✕ DON'T

Don't change the font. Poppins 800 only — no substitutions.

06 — Voice & Tone

How we sound

Our voice is consistent — our tone adapts. A landing page hero is confident and direct. A support email is warm and helpful. Same brand, different register.

📣
Confident
We make claims and back them up. No "we might", "possibly", or "could help". Own the result.
✂️
Direct
Contractors don't have time for fluff. Lead with the point. Short sentences. Active verbs.
🤝
Peer-to-Peer
Talk like a colleague who happens to know marketing — not a salesperson reading a script.
🔢
Specific
"47 calls in 30 days" beats "lots of calls". Numbers create credibility. Vague claims don't.

Writing Do's & Don'ts

Write this
Get 15–40 exclusive job calls per month in your territory.
Your area might already be taken — check availability now.
Real calls. Real homeowners. No shared leads.
We work with HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and roofing contractors.
If calls don't come, you don't pay. Simple as that.
Not this
We leverage synergistic lead generation solutions for your business.
Hurry! Limited time offer! Act now before it's too late!
We might be able to potentially help grow your customer base.
Our platform utilizes cutting-edge AI to optimize your pipeline.
We provide comprehensive multi-channel marketing services.
07 — Content Pillars

Building Authority Through Content

Every piece of content should live inside one of these five pillars. If it doesn't fit, question whether to make it.

01
Proof & Results

Real numbers from real contractors. The most powerful authority signal is someone who looks like your audience saying "this worked." Every result you share builds the case before a prospect even reaches your sales page.

Contractor case studies Monthly call volume screenshots Before/after revenue comparisons Testimonial clips Call recording breakdowns
02
Education & Expertise

Show you understand the trades better than any generic agency. Educate contractors on how lead generation works, what questions to ask vendors, what metrics matter. Teaching trust is the fastest path to buying trust.

How we source calls (no BS breakdown) What makes a good call vs. a bad lead Why shared leads kill close rates Cost-per-job math for each trade Seasonal demand patterns by market
03
Exclusivity & Urgency

Territory-based exclusivity is the core product differentiator. Content in this pillar makes that real — showing specific cities locking up, contractors securing their market, and the cost of waiting. Urgency built on truth, not manufactured scarcity.

Territory availability updates "[City] just claimed" announcements Why your neighbor gets your calls if you wait Waitlist for sold-out markets
04
Behind the Business

Authority requires a face. Content that shows the humans behind More Job Calls — your process, your standards, your values — builds the trust that polished marketing alone never can. Don't hide the machine; let people see it working.

How we qualify calls before they hit your phone Our vetting process for contractors Day in the life / operations content Founder perspective on the trades industry
05
Industry POV & Hot Takes

The trades industry is full of bad marketing advice. Take a stand. Agree with what works, call out what doesn't. Opinions that resonate with contractors position More Job Calls as the voice they trust — not just a vendor they use.

Why most contractor marketing fails The problem with shared lead marketplaces What every plumber should know about Google Responding to industry news or trends
